Hello Lovely Readers !

As J.K. Rowling has rightly said ,"I do believe something very magical can happen when you read a good book ." As the title suggests " My book space " here I'll be posting honest reviews about the books I've read and what are my opinions regarding it and whether you should read it or not . So , keep growing and keep reading !!

Wings of Fire

by APJ Abdul Kalam with Arun Tiwari

An autobiography of a great scientist and a revolutionary individual ; Wings of Fire , is a perfect book which throws light to the life of a middle class boy APJ Abdul Kalam who brought viability to Indian science and lead ISRO to new heights with his sheer dedication and hardwork inspite of numerous complexities and hindrances. It's an appropriate book of motivation for people who think they can't dream big . This book will help you dream big and will let you thrive for it .

Kalam Sir in his words said ,

"We are all born with a divine fire in us. Our efforts should be to give wings to this fire and fill the world with the glow of its goodness . "

My experiments with truth

by Mahatma Gandhi

The book "My experiments with truth " an autobiography of one of the eminent leader and an ascetic " Mahatma Gandhi " starting from his childhood describes the complete life of Gandhi . It breaks the commonly presumed conception about Gandhi being a pious person abstaining himself from worldly attractions since birth . It shows how Gandhi being a normal person like us changes into an altruistic being leaving a profound impact worldwide evolving into a Mahatma and the hardships faced by him . This book very well portrays how our inner self always interrupt us from doing anything erroneous but only he who prioritise his inner self has the ability to bring a change and inspire others .

One of his quotes which has left an indelible impact on me ,

" If I believe I cannot do something , it makes me incapable of doing it. But when I believe I can , then I acquire the ability to do it even If I didn't have it in the beginning".

This book is a must read if you are an adulator of Gandhi , some people may find it monotonous at times but overall it's one of an unprecedented autobiography .

Calling Sehmat

by Harinder Sikka

So, this book is not only about an Indian Spy who puts her life in risk for the country saving it from a catastrophic event but also about what challenges she has to face to do such a job and still be the one who goes unsung and is seldom remembered . A biography which lays out the memoir of an ordinary middle class North Indian girl who takes up her progenitor's profession for the love of her country and family and how she saves her country from a huge mishap , consequently , gambling with her own life.This book also unveils how being involved in such a work can metamorphose your whole life , makes you sacrifice your feelings and why you can never be the same ever again .

This book has also turned into a motion picture named "Raazi" which very briefly describes Sehmat's life . What fascinated me the most after watching the movie which led me reading this appreciable biography is how a shy and timid girl being afraid of ammunitions turns into a fearless and patriotic spy . This moving life story will leave you with an everlasting impression and let you question yourself what are you doing to contribute to your nation . Also , it will make you feel proud of such great unsung patriots.

You must read this book if you are looking for a story which goes deep into the intricacies in the life of a spy and make you realise why such people are worth venerating !

The last lecture by Randy Pausch with Jeffrey Zaslow

by Randy Pausch with Jeffrey Zaslow

We often are very fed up with our lives and tend to search for something that gives us a new ray of hope , which makes us believe that there's so much more to this world , This book "The last lecture " is about the same , which recounts the life of an award winning teacher and researcher , Randy Pausch , who inspite of having every odds in his favour chose to be happy and kept others around him gratified . We find him as a man who has great affection for his family and how he left no stones unturned to keep them happy , despite of suffering from a critical illness as precarious as Cancer and knowing his life would end soon ,eventually leaving his beautiful family of 3 children and his wife . His last lecture at Carnegie Mellon University is available on internet and clearly exhibits his cheerful nature and optimistic personality . No doubt , being in such a egregious condition and still managing to keep yourself happy is the most difficult thing a person can think of and that's really inspiring and worth getting appreciated . But what i felt while reading this book , while in some pages we get really inspired and moved by his audacious words . Other times , it went too dreary.

If you are one of those readers who wants every page of the book to be something that doesn't stultifies you and leaves an impact this book is not for you . On the contrary , if you are one of those readers who is not concerned in how much you got to learn from a single book you can definitely go and read this book !

As i mentioned in some areas there are really impacting words from this zestful individual , i would like to quote one of the many ,

" Follow your passions, believe in karma, and you won't have to chase your dreams, they will come to you. "

Ignited Minds

by APJ Abdul Kalam

It's one of the many books written by this amazing man. This is not one of those books in which he shares about how he , along with his team successfully completed launching rockets and stuff. But more about his experiences and interaction with students and his belief in how these young minds have the capability of changing the world.There's not much description about things in this book as it's a short one and emphasizes more on young minds and Kalam's initiative to enlighten them. Like all of his books this one also isn't the one where you will read and think " Oh ! I haven't learnt anything new ". You'll invariably gain something when it comes to Sir Kalam elucidating his experiences and thoughts . There's always some piece of highly impressive words in a book that leaves me awestruck , quoting one of those from the author ,

"For great men, religion is a way of making friends; small people make religion a fighting tool."

You should definitely read this book it instills how each one of us has the capability of collectively making big changes and how we can see this world with a different and broader perspective!
